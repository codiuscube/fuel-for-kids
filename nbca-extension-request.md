# Email to Nanette — request to extend the penalty-free withdrawal deadline past July 15

## Main message

Hi Nanette,

Thank you again for all your flexibility through this — and thank you, sincerely, for the scholarships for Cassius, Dorothy, and Sebastian. We accepted all three back on June 12, but I know you're absolutely swamped right now, so I wanted to repeat it here in case it got buried: it genuinely means a great deal to our family. I also grabbed a short slot on your calendar for Friday so we can talk through this email together.

There are two things I'm hoping we can work through. The first is our payment plan. Since TEFA funding — if it reaches us — won't arrive until August at the earliest, would the school be open to moving our FACTS account to a TEFA-aligned schedule, with payments beginning in August and timed to the disbursements? I know that's a special request outside the normal schedule, and I'd also love to understand what August would actually look like in practice, since TEFA still hasn't published a firm date for that first 25% disbursement.

The second is the bigger one — the last piece of our puzzle.

You've confirmed that June 30 is the date we'd need to decide by to withdraw penalty-free, and that after that it's a 10% penalty in July (~$4,800) and 20% in August. We completely understand the policy. The hard part for us is purely the calendar: the largest TEFA waitlist event of the year — the July 15 opt-in/opt-out deadline, when the biggest wave of awarded families drops out and seats cascade down the list — falls about two weeks *after* June 30. The waitlist is already visibly moving — Tier 2 families are being awarded week over week — but nothing reaches a Tier 3 band like ours until that July 15 wave, if at all. So as things stand, we'd have to make a final, penalty-bearing decision on June 30 without seeing the one piece of information most likely to change it.

My ask: would the school be willing to extend our penalty-free withdrawal date to July 31 (even July 20 — just past the July 15 cascade — would do it)? That would let us see whether anything reaches our band before we commit, instead of deciding blind two weeks early.

One thing I hope makes this easier to consider: if we're able to move to the August-start schedule I asked about above, the school wouldn't have collected any tuition from us before that late-July window anyway — so the extension wouldn't change any payment timing on your end, only the date the penalty would attach.

I want to be candid about what's at stake, because I'd rather say it plainly now than surprise you on June 30. We really want all three of our kids at NBCA and we want to partner with the school — that is our first choice by far. But we genuinely can't yet see how we afford all three children, or carry the penalty risk for all three, if we're forced to commit two weeks before the one event that could change the math. So without the breathing room of an extension, we won't be able to enroll all three — we'd have no responsible choice but to make a withdrawal decision by June 30, not because we want to, but because committing blind to all three is more than we can take on. An extension is what lets us keep all three kids in the school while we wait for the one piece of information that matters. If it isn't something the school can do, we completely understand, and we'll make our decision by June 30.

Hopefully sending this ahead of Friday makes the call easier to have. Thank you so much for continuing to work through this with us — it means a great deal.

Mahalo,
Cody Iddings
