# Email to Nanette — request to extend the penalty-free withdrawal deadline past July 15

## Main message

Hi Nanette,

Thank you again for all your flexibility through this. Two quick things — one is a gentle follow-up, the other a new request.

First, just circling back on my last note: we'd love to take you up on your offer to move us to the TEFA-aligned schedule with payments beginning in August (private pay spread out, lump sums timed to disbursements). No rush at all — I know you're swamped — I just wanted to make sure that request didn't slip through. We've accepted the scholarships for all three kids and we're committed to having them at NBCA.

Second, a new request in the same spirit, and it's the last piece of our puzzle.

You've confirmed that June 30 is the date we'd need to decide by to withdraw penalty-free, and that after that it's a 10% penalty in July (~$4,800) and 20% in August. We completely understand the policy. The hard part for us is purely the calendar: the largest TEFA waitlist event of the year — the July 15 opt-in/opt-out deadline, when the biggest wave of awarded families drops out and seats cascade down the list — falls about two weeks *after* June 30. So as things stand, we'd have to make a final, penalty-bearing decision on June 30 without seeing the one piece of information most likely to change it.

My ask: would the school be willing to extend our penalty-free withdrawal date to July 31 (even July 20 — just past the July 15 cascade — would do it)? That would let us see whether anything reaches our band before we commit, instead of deciding blind two weeks early.

One thing that I hope makes this easier to consider: if we're able to move to the August-start schedule I asked about above, the school wouldn't have collected any tuition from us before that late-July window anyway — so the extension wouldn't change any payment timing on your end, only the date the penalty would attach.

I want to be really clear that this isn't us hedging away from NBCA. We want our kids there, we've accepted the scholarships, and if you grant the extension our plan is simple: ride out July 15, and if nothing changes — which is the likely outcome, as you and we both expect for a Tier 3 family — we stay and pay. We just don't want to be forced into a ~$4,800 penalty by a two-week mismatch that's entirely outside our control. If an extension isn't something the school can do, we completely understand, and we'll make our decision by June 30.

Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier. Thank you so much for continuing to work through this with us — it means a great deal.

Mahalo,
