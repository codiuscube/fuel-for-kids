# FB reply to Anonymous participant 754 / supporting Krissy Young

Krissy, you're right — and the math is sharper than "around 15%." Per the Apr 28 Lottery Update PDF: Tier 1 + siblings used ~$415M of the $1B. After ~$55M in program overhead (Odyssey admin fee at the SB 2 5% cap + appeals reserve), ~$530M funds Tier 2 — that's ~62k of the 72,927 T2 applicants (~85%) at the typical 77/23 private/homeschool setting mix. Only ~10k unfunded T2 sit ahead of T3.

At 15% attrition: T3 ~10% per child, ~27% for a family of 3 (sibling rule). At 25% attrition (Milwaukee/Virginia precedent): ~27% / ~62%. Materially in play, not a long shot.

Two quick flags on the comptroller convo:

- The Parent Application Guide (page 4) says "Participants who remain in good standing will not need to reapply each year" — so annual reenrollment isn't required.
- The annual-IEP-update claim isn't in any of the official PDFs either; worth re-asking Odyssey to confirm.
