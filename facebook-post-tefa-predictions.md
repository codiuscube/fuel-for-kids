Been tracking the TEFA process and waitlist numbers pretty closely since our kids landed in the 30,001-50,000 band, so here's where I think this actually goes. Not trying to give anyone false hope or doom, just the math as I see it.

As of the June 10 press release: 100k+ students funded, about 2,000 opt-outs so far, and every new award is still going to Tier 2 families. There were 20,383 Tier 2 kids on the waitlist when the lottery ran, and roughly 7,400 of them have been funded since. So Tier 3 hasn't moved at all yet, and it can't until that whole Tier 2 line clears.

My safe guess: Tier 2 finishes clearing mid to late July, right around the July 15 deadline when every awarded family has to confirm a school, take the $2,000 homeschool option, or opt out. That's when the real shakeout happens. After that I think offers reach down to around position 31,400, with actual funded seats closer to 27,500.

So if your number is under 25k, I think you're in good shape. 25-30k, possible. Past 30k (where we are), it's a long shot this year. But my aggressive take is that towards the end of July, we are going to see waitlist numbers 50,000+. Why? It's because of the Reserve Budget they put aside.

That's the part we need to focus on: where is the money for these new awards even coming from? Three places.
1. Families opting out completely, which frees their whole award (and a Tier 1 award with disability supplements can be worth a lot more than the base $10,500, so one of those can fund 2-3 seats).
2. Families switching to the $2,000 homeschool option, which frees about $8,500 each.
3. And the appeals reserve, which is money the Comptroller held back to pay appeals that get granted. Whatever doesn't get used on appeals eventually flows down to the waitlist.

That third one is the wildcard. They've never said how big it is, but you can back into it with their own numbers. The budget is $1 billion. A spokesperson told Community Impact that about $820M is set aside for the ~96,000 accepted students. Admin and vendor costs are capped at $80M by law. So there's at least $100M sitting in that reserve. That's enough for roughly 11,700 more seats if every dollar of it released. It won't all release, because granted appeals and special ed supplements get paid out of it first (the 477 special ed awards in late May already came from there). But most of the appeals will need to be finalised this week.

My numbers above only assume about $25M of it reaches the waitlist. But that gap between $25M and $100M is the single biggest unknown in anyone's odds, and it's the thing they've communicated the least about.

If the next Comptroller update shows a big jump in awards WITHOUT a matching jump in opt-outs, that's the reserve releasing, and the size of that batch tells you how big it really is.

(All my own math from public Comptroller releases, happy to share sources. Final calls are made by the Comptroller, not me.)
