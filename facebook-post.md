🚨 TEFA Update — the real numbers are out, and they look better than what's been going around.

Quick take: Tier 2 is in good shape. Tier 3 is a long shot, but it's no longer a "no shot." Here's what the Comptroller's April 28 figures actually say.

**Tier 1 — done.**
42,642 kids awarded: 27,050 with disabilities and 15,592 siblings. That's about $415M of the $1B Year 1 budget already committed.

**Tier 2 (under 200% FPL) — happening this week.**
That leaves roughly $585M. SB 2 caps total admin at 8% (3% Comptroller + 5% program administrator), so I'm setting aside about $85M for admin and an appeals reserve. That puts ~$500M into Tier 2.

The Comptroller's applicant mix is 77% private school / 23% homeschool-other. Run the math on that mix and ~$500M funds about 58,651 of the 72,927 Tier 2 applicants — roughly 80%. Even the all-private worst case lands near 65%. So the 30–40% figures some groups have been sharing look too low.

**Tier 3 (200–500% FPL, no IEP) — only via cascade.**
Tier 3 isn't in the initial allocation. It only opens if T1 and T2 awards go unused, and any leftovers first roll back to remaining T2 applicants. T3 only starts moving once T2 is cleared.

Using my central 15% attrition estimate, Tier 3 could see some movement — though less than I'd posted before, once the full 8% admin cap is accounted for:

- ~4.6% chance per child
- ~13.2% for a family of 3 using the sibling rule

At 25% attrition it gets a lot more interesting:

- ~22% per child
- ~53% for a family of 3

**Why attrition could matter.**
A few reasons stack on top of each other:

- Comparable programs saw meaningful drop-off (D.C. 14.3%, Milwaukee 30%, Virginia 20–34%).
- The $10,474 award doesn't fully cover tuition at a lot of private schools.
- Many private schools required signed contracts before TEFA notifications even started.
- Funding rolls out in waves — only about $2,600 hits accounts by July 1, but plenty of tuition deposits are due before that.
- Some T1 awardees are already saying they can't use the funds because of school fit, participating-school availability, or IEP/documentation issues.

**Bottom line.** Tier 2 is looking solid. Tier 3 isn't likely, but it's not impossible either. The April 28 numbers turn this into a real cascade question instead of a flat "no shot."
