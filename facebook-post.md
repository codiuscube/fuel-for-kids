🚨 TEFA Update — The real numbers are out, and they're still better than what's going around. I believe T3 students have a shot, but it is very uncertain.
In case you missed it: the Comptroller released official figures on April 28. Here's what they actually say:

TIER 1 — COMPLETE
42,642 kids have been awarded — 27,050 with disabilities and 15,592 siblings. That's roughly $415M of the $1B Year 1 budget committed.

TIER 2 (under 200% FPL) — HAPPENING THIS WEEK
After Tier 1, about $585M remains. SB 2 allows up to 8% total admin costs (3% Comptroller + 5% program administrator), so I am now modeling the conservative admin/reserve number as ~$85M. That leaves about ~$500M for Tier 2.

Based on the Comptroller's applicant mix of 77% private school / 23% homeschool-other, that funds about 58,651 of 72,927 Tier 2 applicants — around 80%. Even the all-private conservative floor is about 65%. So the 30–40% numbers some groups shared still look too low.

TIER 3 (200–500% FPL, no IEP) — NOT IN THE INITIAL LOTTERY
Tier 3 would only be reached through a cascade. Any unused awards first go to the remaining Tier 2 applicants, and only after Tier 2 is cleared would spots begin moving into Tier 3.

My central 15% attrition estimate suggests Tier 3 could see some movement, but less than I originally posted after accounting for the full 8% admin cap:
~4.6% chance per child
~13.2% for a family of 3 using the sibling rule

At 25% attrition, it becomes much stronger:
~22% per child
~53% for a family of 3

WHY ATTRITION COULD MATTER
Past programs in other states had meaningful drop-off
The $10,474 award doesn't cover full tuition at many private schools
Lots of private schools required signed contracts before TEFA notifications started
Funding rolls out in waves — only ~$2,600 hits accounts by July 1, but many tuition deposits are due earlier
Some T1 awardees already report they can't use the funds because of school fit, participating-school availability, or IEP/document issues

Bottom line: T2 still looks strong. T3 is not likely, but it is not impossible either. The April 28 numbers make it a real cascade question, not a "no shot" situation.
