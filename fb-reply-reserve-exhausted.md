# FB reply — "the reserve fund money has already been used" (Odyssey support claim)

*Reply to the OP who was told by an Odyssey agent that the reserve is gone and only opt-outs remain. Cody's voice. Plain text on purpose — Facebook won't render markdown.*

---

I'd take that "the reserve has already been used" line with a grain of salt — I don't think it holds up against the program's own updates, and I think the agent mixed up two different things.

Here's why. Every single award announcement so far tells you where the money came from, and it's never been appeals. May 4, June 10, and the June 23 update all say the new awards were funded by families opting out or switching to homeschool/other ($10,474 drops to $2,000, freeing about $8,500 each). That's recycled award money — a completely separate bucket from the appeals reserve. If the reserve had actually been releasing, they'd have said so, and you'd see a big jump in awards without a matching jump in opt-outs. We haven't seen that yet.

On the size: you can back into the reserve from their own numbers. $1B total, a spokesperson told Community Impact about $820M is set aside for the ~96,000 first-wave students, and admin/vendor is capped at $80M by law. That leaves roughly $100M in reserve. For that to be "used up," you'd need something like $88M+ of granted appeals — thousands of them — and the program has never announced anything close. On top of that, when an appeal is granted, the family usually just gets placed on the waitlist in lottery order, not paid out immediately. So appeals don't burn the reserve the way it sounds.

My read: the agent almost certainly meant "fully committed/obligated," not "actually spent." From a support screen the whole pot looks allocated — first-wave awards plus the reserve earmarked for appeals — so there's "nothing free to hand out today." That's not the same as the reserve being gone.

And here's the part that matters for you: the Comptroller has said the full $1B is committed to Year 1. If that's true, the unused reserve legally can't roll into next year — so whatever appeals don't consume has to cascade down to the waitlist this year. The natural trigger is the July 15 opt-in deadline, once appeals are finalized and declines shake out.

So I wouldn't treat "the reserve is gone" as the final word. Watch for the one signal that actually proves it either way: awards jumping without opt-outs rising. That batch size is what finally tells us whether the reserve was $25M or $100M.

(All my own math from public Comptroller releases — happy to share sources. Final calls are the Comptroller's, not mine.)
