🚨 TEFA Waitlist Update — what your bucket actually means

A lot of you got your waitlist position this week. The Comptroller is communicating positions in approximate buckets (precise positions only for families ranked ≤ 25,000), so I wanted to share what each bucket realistically needs for an offer to reach you. The bands below are grounded in the May 7 Lottery Update PDF and the May 12 Waitlist Information PDF.

**Quick framework**

- About 53,300 students were awarded in the first wave (Tier 1 + Tier 2).
- The waitlist is roughly 152,700 students — Tier 2 (~20,400) first, then Tier 3 (~65,400), then Tier 4 (~67,000).
- Spots only free up through three things: families not opting in by July 15, families switching to homeschool/other (funding drops from ~$10,474 to $2,000), and the **appeals reserve** being released. (The appeals reserve is funding the Comptroller holds back to pay out successful tier and eligibility appeals — families who challenge their placement and win. As those appeals resolve over May/June, the unused dollars cascade back to the regular waitlist.)
- Awards cascade in strict tier order. Every unfunded Tier 2 spot clears before any Tier 3 offer goes out.

**My baseline assumption.** For everything below, I'm assuming non-participation lands at roughly **15% on Tier 1 awards, 18% on first-wave Tier 2 awards, and 35% on late replacement offers** — a blend that matches the 14–34% range seen in D.C., Milwaukee, and Virginia. I'm also assuming **about $25M of the inferred $100M+ appeals reserve actually cascades to the regular waitlist** (the rest gets consumed by successful appeals, SPED set-asides, and admin). Under that baseline the model fully clears Tier 2 and projects roughly **7,150 funded Tier 3 seats with offer-depth out to ~11,000 in Tier 3** (global cutoffs ~27,500 funded / ~31,400 offer).

A quick aside: I believe these are conservative assumptions, but please take them with a grain of salt. This isn't meant to be an optimistic post — it's meant to be grounded in data and honest about the trade-offs. If you disagree with where the math lands, that's fair. The goal is to be hopeful and useful for families facing hard decisions over the next few weeks. My family is in that boat too.

So the question for each band isn't really "what attrition rate?" — at the baseline above, T2 already clears. It's: **what does the band need _in addition_ to that baseline?** So a reminder of the three levers we are looking at:

- Current award / first-wave attrition for Tier 1 (15%) and Tier 2 (18%) families
- Late replacement award attrition for Tier 2/3 familes (35%)
- Appeal reserve coming back into the fold ($25M of $100M)

---

**Tier 2 buckets** (waitlist positions 1 – 20,383)

Under the 15/18/35 + $25M baseline, every Tier 2 bucket is expected to receive offers. The difference between buckets is mostly timing as the cascade rolls out:

- **1 – 1,000** — covered by the appeals reserve alone ($25M is enough to reach roughly the first 5,000 Tier 2 positions on its own) plus the earliest T1/T2 attrition signals. Expected this month.
- **1,001 – 5,000** — expected May/June as initial T1/T2 declines accumulate.
- **5,001 – 10,000** — expected in June.
- **10,001 – 15,000** — expected June/July, especially as the 35% replacement-decline cascade kicks in (early offers go out, ~35% decline, those dollars cascade further).
- **15,001 – 20,000** — expected in July, specifically after the July 15 opt-in deadline triggers the largest cascade event of the year.

If real-world non-participation lands materially below 15/18/35 — i.e. private-school families have unusually high follow-through compared to other programs — the upper Tier 2 buckets are where the math gets tight.

---

**Tier 3 buckets** (global waitlist 20,384 – 85,750)

- **20,001 – 25,000** — already inside the modeled funded-seat count at baseline. No additional levers needed. Expected July, conditional on Tier 2 clearing on schedule.
- **25,001 – 30,000** — top half of this band is past funded-seat depth but still inside offer-depth at baseline. An offer is expected at baseline; converting it to a funded seat across the full band needs about **+$20M of additional appeals reserve** (so $45M total, vs. the $25M baseline) or a small attrition bump. Possible in July.
- **30,001 – 50,000** — past modeled offer-depth at baseline. Reaching an offer in the bottom half of this band (ranks ~30,001–40,000) needs roughly **+$20–50M of additional appeals reserve** (so $45–75M total — well within the inferred $100M+ pool), or decline rates scaled by ~1.1–1.2x (T1 ~16–18% / T2 first-wave ~20–22% / replacement ~38–42%) — a modest step up from baseline. Reaching even an offer at the top end of this band (rank 50,000) needs roughly **+$100M of additional appeals reserve** cascading to the regular waitlist (so $125M total, which approaches the entire inferred reserve pool) — OR all three decline rates scaled by ~1.7x (T1 ~26% / T2 first-wave ~31% / replacement ~60%, which is historical-high territory). **Maybe some of this band but mostly not expected in Year 1.**
- **50,001 – 100,000** — even pushing both levers to their structural caps (95% declines + the full inferred $100M+ reserve) the model can't reach the back half of Tier 3 in Year 1. **Not expected.**

**Tier 4 buckets** — not expected in Year 1. Tier 4 only opens after Tier 3 fully clears, and the model can't get there even with both levers maxed.

---

**What to do if you're in a Tier 3 bucket like us**

1. Don't budget around TEFA arriving in Year 1. If it cascades to you, treat it as upside, not your plan.
2. Watch the post-July 15 window — that's when most realistic Tier 3 movement happens, after the August-funding track confirmations.
3. Year 2 is the bigger swing. The $1B is for the full 2025-2027 biennium, and a lot of variables (T1 IEP pool growth, legislative changes, public-school re-enrollment) reset.

For the Tier 2 families — there's real cause for hope. Most of you are getting funded; even in the higher buckets, the July 15 cascade is a genuine event, not a wishful one.

For the Tier 3 families — I'm right there with you. The math is hard, but the families who got T1 and T2 are real families who needed it.
