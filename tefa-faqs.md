### Student Eligibility and Grade Placement
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/50085002752795-Student-Eligibility-and-Grade-Placement*

**If my child qualifies for TEFA, does their age have to match their grade placement?**
No. A student may still receive TEFA funds even if their grade placement does not match the grade level typically associated with their age.

For example, a student may be placed in a different grade level based on their academic needs or readiness.

**What if my child is eligible for Kindergarten, but I want them to attend Pre-K?**
If your child turns five years old by September 1, they are eligible for Kindergarten. However, you may choose to enroll your child in either Pre-K or Kindergarten based on what best supports their needs and readiness.

Families can choose the learning environment that best supports their child's academic and developmental needs.

### TEFA Parent Appeals Process
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/49194996729115-TEFA-Parent-Appeals-Process*

_This article explains the appeal process for the TEFA program._

## **Overview**

Parents have the right to appeal a decision made on behalf of their students. This may include eligibility, prioritization, or funding decisions.

We encourage parents to reach out to Odyssey support with any questions or issues. If a parent does not agree with a particular outcome, they may initiate an appeal.

## **How to Initiate an Appeal**

Parents have **30 days** from outcome notification to initiate the appeal process. To do so, a parent must contact Odyssey support requesting the appeal and include the information as to the basis of the appeal. Once received, if the situation warrants an appeal, the support agent will initiate the process.

### Private School Meals in the TEFA Program
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/47965899621403-Private-School-Meals-in-the-TEFA-Program*

Private school meals such as breakfast and lunch are eligible expenses for the TEFA Program. For TEFA families these expenses are handled directly through your school rather than through the Odyssey marketplace.

### **How to Purchase Meals**

If your student attends a participating private school, meal costs can be paid through the school portal. In addition to paying tuition, parents can send direct payments to their school for additional purchases such as meals and transportation.

By purchasing meals directly through your school, you can:

  * Bundle meal costs alongside tuition or other fees
  * Ensure charges are applied to your student's account
  * Experience a simpler, more streamlined payment process

### **Why Meals Aren't Available in the Marketplace**

The Odyssey marketplace is designed for broadly available educational goods and services. Since school meals are specific to enrolled students and typically managed by the school itself, they are not listed as marketplace items.

### **What This Means for Families**

  * Meals are eligible educational expense when provided by your student's school
  * You should purchase meals directly through your school, not through the marketplace

### Adding IEP to Student Application
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/46282153065243-Adding-IEP-to-Student-Application*

_This article is intended for parents applying to the TEFA program who need to upload IEP information_

### Adding an IEP

If you have an IEP on file with Texas Education Agency (TEA) for either the **2023-2024, 2024-2025, or 2025-2026** school year (a current IEP per Comptroller materials), you may be eligible to receive additional funding.

If this situation is applicable to your child, you will be prompted to input a Texas Unique Student Identifier, or UID, at the bottom of the student application page. This UID will be used to validate your child's IEP with TEA directly.

No document uploads are necessary for this process.

_[Screenshot: UID input field]_

In the event your child does not have an IEP on file with TEA for the 2023-2024 school year or more recently, you will be prompted to upload either a Disability Certification Form, FIIE, out-of-state IEP, or expired Texas IEP in order to validate your child's qualifying disability. This may make your child eligible for increased priority, but will not receive additional funding.

_[Screenshot: Disability documentation upload]_

If you have any additional questions please contact our Customer Support.

### Where Can a Parent Find Their Child's Texas Student Unique ID (UID) Number if They Do Not Have It?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/46087293538331-Where-Can-a-Parent-Find-Their-Child-s-Texas-Student-Unique-ID-UID-Number-if-They-Do-Not-Have-It*

The source of truth for Texas Student Unique Identifiers (also known as a UID) is through your public school district (ISD) in which the IEP was created. Your district should have this identifier on record even if your student has been attending private school.

Alternatively, parents of students who have ever attended a public school can visit this [website](<https://tealprod.tea.state.tx.us/Tea.Widgets/Student/?qid=21&ref=>) to look up their student's UID. Click on the "Student Look Up" button at the top center of the page which will open a screen where you can submit your child's first name, social security number, and date of birth. If they have been registered in a public school, the website will provide their UID number.

Currently, this portal does not contain UID lookups for private school students with ISD IEPs. Private school student parents should contact the special education department of the ISD that completed your student's IEP.

If your child has only attended private school, your local public school district should be able to enter your student's IEP information into TEA's APEX system on your behalf. This will allow them to provide you with a UID.

If you have exhausted all methods of obtaining a UID, then you may enter "11111" followed by your zip code in the application instead of the Texas Unique Student Identifier. This will allow you to continue with the application. However, please note that the only way to ensure additional funding for a child with a disability is to have a validated IEP on file with TEA, which requires the child to have a valid Texas Unique Student Identifier (or UID). Please note that the only way to ensure additional SPED funding for TEFA is to have a validated IEP on file with TEA, which means the child has a valid Texas Unique Student Identifier (or UID).

For additional information on what special education criteria is applicable for additional funding and priority versus what special education criteria is applicable for only priority, please [read this article](<https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44029513305755-I-Have-a-Child-with-a-Disability-What-Do-I-Need-to-Do>).

### Who Can Complete the TEFA Disability Certification Form?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45997909281179-Who-Can-Complete-the-TEFA-Disability-Certification-Form*

The TEFA Disability Certification Form may be completed by a licensed professional who is qualified to certify a child's disability.

A licensed professional is someone who holds an active credential issued by a state licensing or certifying authority and is legally permitted to diagnose or certify a disability within their scope of practice.

Depending on the disability category, this may include:

  * Physician
  * Nurse practitioner
  * Physician assistant
  * Licensed psychologist or licensed school psychologist
  * Licensed therapist, including speech language pathologist, occupational therapist, or physical therapist
  * Licensed audiologist or optometrist
  * Certified teacher or educational professional, when the disability category allows certification by an educator

The professional should only sign the form if the disability falls within their scope of license, certification, or role. Some disability categories require certification by a specific specialty or by more than one licensed professional.

For visual impairments, the form requires three signatures: a licensed ophthalmologist or optometrist, a certified teacher of students with visual impairments, and a certified orientation and mobility specialist.

You only need to submit one form. You do not need to certify more than one disability.

If your child already has a current IEP on file with the Texas Education Agency, you do not need to submit this form.

The Disability Certification Form is used only to determine priority in the TEFA application process. It does not create an IEP and does not affect funding amounts.

### Can Active-Duty Military Families Moving to Texas Participate?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45749124676251-Can-Active-Duty-Military-Families-Moving-to-Texas-Participate*

_This article is intended non-resident, Active-Duty parents and guardians moving to Texas that want to apply for the Texas Education Freedom Accounts Program._

If you are an active duty military family moving to Texas you may participate in TEFA as long as your family will be in Texas prior to the start of the 2026-2027 school year.

On your application, you will be required to enter a Texas based address. If you do not have your housing address yet, you may enter your duty station address for now. Please make sure to update this address prior to the school year starting within the Odyssey system with your actual residence.

You will also be prompted to upload active duty orders as part of your application process. The Odyssey team will review your orders to ensure accuracy and that they place you within a duty station in the state of Texas.

### What Do the Texas IEP Instructional Codes Mean?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45446233534875-What-Do-the-Texas-IEP-Instructional-Codes-Mean*

_This article helps Texas families understand what each Individualized Education Program (IEP) instructional code means, so they can select the correct one when applying._

When applying, families with an active Texas Individualized Education Program (IEP) on file with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) will be asked to select an instructional code number from a dropdown menu. To choose the correct number, you'll need to match the code shown in your child's IEP.

Below is a list of instructional code numbers and what each one means:

**Code**| **Description**
---|---
00| No instructional setting (such as Speech Therapy)
01| Homebound

### What is The S-Number or Texas Unique Child Identification Number?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45352226587547-What-is-The-S-Number-or-Texas-Unique-Child-Identification-Number*

_This article is intended to help Texas families understand the S-Number and the Texas Unique Child Identification Number for their child._

### **S-Number** :

Your child may have an S-Number if they were enrolled in or received program services from a Texas public school district or charter school without a Social Security number.

The S-Number:

  * Is used to validate the child's Individualized Education Program (IEP), prekindergarten eligibility, and/or previous public school enrollment information (if applicable).
  * Begins with an S and is followed by 8 numeric characters.


### **Texas Unique Child Identification Number** :

The Texas Unique Child Identification Number is a unique ID used by the Texas Education Agency (TEA). A child will have a Texas Unique Child Identification Number if they have ever been enrolled in a Texas public school or charter school, or if they have a registered IEP/ISP on file.

### What is TEFA?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44028942090907-What-is-TEFA*

_This article is intended for Texas parents to provide more information about the Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA) program._

The TEFA program was established by the Texas Legislature in 2025 to give parents the freedom to choose the best education path for their child's future. Beginning with the 2026–27 school year, this program will provide participating families with education savings accounts that can be used for approved educational expenses.

Families accepted into the program will be able to direct their funds toward private school tuition, educational expenses for homeschoolers, tutoring, career and technical education programs, and more.

The TEFA Program will be administered by the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts, with Odyssey serving as the certified educational assistance organization responsible for helping families apply, access their accounts, and pay for approved educational expenses.

### Who is Eligible?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44029154424603-Who-is-Eligible*

_This article is intended for Texas parents to help them understand the eligibility requirements for the Texas Education Freedom Accounts program._

To qualify for the program, a student must:

  * Be a citizen or national of the United States or lawfully admitted into the United States.
  * Be eligible to attend a Texas public school or open-enrollment charter school, including children of a parent who is an active-duty member of the U.S. armed forces, or eligible to enroll in a Texas public or open-enrollment charter school's free prekindergarten program.
  * Live in the state of Texas.

### What Prekindergarten Students are Eligible for the Program?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44029943492891-What-Prekindergarten-Students-are-Eligible-for-the-Program*

_This article is intended for Texas parents to understand how their prekindergarten student would qualify for the Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA) Program._

In order to be eligible for TEFA as a prekindergarten student, the child must be 3 to 5 years old as of September 1st of the upcoming school year. Additionally, they must be eligible to enroll in a Texas public or open-enrollment charter school’s free prekindergarten program.

Please note that participation in the Early Childhood Special Education is **NOT** an eligibility criteria for participation.

This means that **one** of the below situations must apply:

### 1\. Student qualifies for free or reduced lunch (also referred to as Educationally Disadvantaged)

A child qualifies as educationally disadvantaged if their household income meets the requirements for the National School Lunch Program (NSLP) or if the child automatically qualifies through certain assistance programs (such as SNAP or TANF). The income limit for NSLP is at or below 185% of the Federal Poverty Line. Income information will be collected in Odyssey for validation.

### 2\. Emerging Bilingual (Emergent Bilingual)

A child qualifies as Emerging (Emergent) Bilingual if they are unable to speak and understand English well enough to fully participate in an English-language classroom.

### 3\. Parent is Active Duty Military

A child qualifies if at least one parent or legal guardian is currently serving on active duty in the U.S. Armed Forces (Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, or Coast Guard), the Texas National Guard when activated, or a U.S. military reserve component while activated, and the child resides in Texas.

### 4\. One Parent is Military MIA / Injured or Killed in the Line of Duty

A child qualifies if a parent or guardian is a member of the U.S. Armed Forces who is missing in action (MIA) or was injured or killed while serving on active duty. This eligibility category recognizes families impacted by military service-related sacrifice. For validation, the parent will be required to upload either a statement of service, line of duty determination documentation, Purple Heart orders or applicable citations.

### 5\. Foster Care

A child qualifies if they currently live in Texas and are in foster care, or were previously in foster care in Texas or another state or U.S. territory. Once a child qualifies under foster care status, they remain eligible for prekindergarten even if they later exit foster care. For validation, the parent will be required to either upload court order documents, adoption paperwork, DFPS foster/kinship placement documents, PreK verification letter or other valid proof that the child is in foster care

### 6\. Homeless

A child qualifies if the child is experiencing homelessness, regardless of where the child or family is temporarily living. This includes children without a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence.

### 7\. Parent is a Star of Texas Recipient

A child qualifies if a parent has been officially recognized as a Star of Texas Award recipient between 2004 and the current award year. This award honors first responders who were killed or seriously injured in the line of duty. Eligibility is verified using the official Star of Texas resolution or certificate. For validation, the parent will be required to upload the applicable Star of Texas certificate.

### 8\. Parent is a teacher at the school in which the child is eligible to enroll

A child is eligible for enrollment in a prekindergarten class under this section if the child is at least three years of age and is the child of a person employed as a classroom teacher at a public primary or secondary school in the school district that offers a prekindergarten class under this section.

### I Have a Child with a Disability: What Do I Need to Do?

_This article is intended for Texas parents of children with disabilities._


For purposes of prioritization, the term “children with a disability” includes both children with an individualized education program (IEP) on file with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) at the time the application process closes and children who have submitted a program-approved proof-of-disability form with their application. However, only those children with an IEP on file who are accepted into the program are eligible for the increased funding amount available to children with a disability.


### Children with an individualized education program (IEP) from the past three years:

Children may qualify for top priority and additional TEFA funding—up to $30,000 per school year—if they (1) have an Individualized Education Program (IEP) on file with the Texas Education Agency (TEA) within the past three years at the time priority is determined, and (2) are part of a household with total annual income at or below 500% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL).

To receive top priority and the additional funding, the child must have or have had an IEP on file with TEA for the **2023–2024, 2024–2025, or 2025–2026** school year (aligned with Comptroller appeals copy on “current IEP”). TEA must return an instructional code from a student's IEP to Odyssey in order for the student to receive funding; a 504 plan does not constitute additional funding or priority for the TEFA program.

To ensure additional funding is received, please confirm that the current public school district that you are zoned for is properly submitting your child's IEP information to TEA as soon as possible. For the best chances of receiving additional special education funding, the IEP must be on file with the Texas Education Agency prior to the application window closing

Please note that during the application process, you will be asked to provide the following:

  * Numerical instructional code (For more information, please review this [article](https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45446233534875-What-Do-the-Texas-IEP-Instructional-Codes-Mean))
  * Student’s next annual review date (This is sometimes referred to as the “next annual ARD date” or the “due date”)
  * Student's Unique Texas Student Identifier number, if applicable (If the Unique Texas Student ID is unknown, please call your local school district)
  * Student's S-number, if applicable
  * Student's social security number
  * Student's date of birth


Helpful article: [What is The S-Number or Texas Unique Child Identification Number?](https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45352226587547-What-is-The-S-Number-or-Texas-Unique-Child-Identification-Number)

###
Children **without** an individualized education program (IEP) from the past three years, but with a qualifying disability:

Children with a qualifying disability who do not have an IEP on file with TEA from the past three years are not eligible for additional funding. However, if the household income is at or below 500% of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL), they may still qualify for placement in the top-priority group for children with disabilities from households at or below 500% FPL. To be considered for this group, please submit one of the following during the application process:

### What Disabilities Qualify a Child for Eligibility in a School District's Special Education Program?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/45187195515163-What-Disabilities-Qualify-a-Child-for-Eligibility-in-a-School-District-s-Special-Education-Program*

_This article is intended to help Texas parents understand the types of disabilities that may make a child eligible for special education services in a Texas public school district, which may impact eligibility for the Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA) Program. These criteria will also be used to prioritize applicable children approved for TEFA._

General definitions for eligible disabilities under TEFA are noted in this article. However, to be prioritized as a child with a disability, the child must have an Individualized Education Program (IEP) from a Texas public school district or from out-of-state, a full individual and initial evaluation (FIIE), or the parent must provide a TEFA Disability Certification Form for the child. A link to the Disability Certification Form required for TEFA is available for download [here](https://educationfreedom.texas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/TEFA-Disability-Form.pdf). Please take this form to your child’s practitioner for completion and signature, and submit it with your online application.


Additional funding is only available to children who have or have had an IEP on file with the Texas Education Agency for the **2023–2024, 2024–2025, or 2025–2026** school year, and whose household income is at or below 500% of the federal poverty level. The other documents mentioned above will be considered only when prioritizing who will receive a TEFA account. A TEFA Disability Certification form or FIIE does not meet the requirements for additional account funding.


The following disabilities generally qualify a child to participate in a school district's special education program:

### **Hearing impairment**

  * **General Definition** : A hearing impairment, either permanent or fluctuating, that adversely affects the child's educational performance or is so severe that a child cannot process linguistic information through hearing, even with amplification, and that adversely affects the child’s educational performance.


###
**Autism**

  * **General Definition** : A developmental disability that significantly affects verbal and nonverbal communication and social interaction, generally evident before the age of three, that adversely affects the child's educational performance. This category can include repetitive behaviors, unusual responses to sensory input, and resistance to changes in routine.


###
**Deaf-blindness**

  * **General Definition** : The combination of hearing and visual impairments that creates severe communication, developmental, and educational needs and adversely affects the child's educational performance. A child with deaf-blindness cannot be accommodated in special education programs designed for only visual or only hearing impairment.


### **Developmental delay**

  * **General Definition:** A child between the ages of 3-9 who is evaluated by a licensed practitioner for at least one disability that adversely affects the child’s educational performance and whose evaluation data indicates a need for special education and related services and shows evidence of, but does not clearly confirm, the presence of the suspected disability due to the child's young age. Developmental delay may occur in physical development, cognitive development, communication development, social or emotional development, or adaptive development, and adversely affects the child’s educational performance.


###
**Emotional disability**

  * **General Definition** : An emotional disturbance with specific characteristics over a long period that adversely affects the child's educational performance, such as an inability to learn not explained by other factors, difficulty building relationships, inappropriate behavior or feelings, pervasive unhappiness or depression, or fears or physical symptoms related to school problems. The condition includes schizophrenia but does not include social maladjustment alone.


###
**Intellectual disability**

  * **General Definition** : Significantly below-average intellectual functioning with deficits in adaptive behavior that appear during the developmental period and adversely affect the child’s educational performance.


###
**Multiple disabilities**

  * **General Definition** : Two or more disabilities (not deaf-blindness) that adversely affect the child’s educational performance and cause severe educational needs requiring a special education program for more than one impairment. Such disabilities must be expected to continue indefinitely and severely impair performance in two or more of the following areas: psychomotor skills, self-care skills, communication, social and emotional development, or cognition.


###
**Orthopedic impairment**

  * **General Definition** : A severe orthopedic impairment that adversely affects the child's education. This includes impairments from birth anomalies, diseases like bone tuberculosis, or other causes such as cerebral palsy or amputations.


###
**Other health impairment**

  * **General Definition** : Limited strength, vitality, or alertness due to chronic or acute health problems that adversely affect the child’s educational performance. This can include conditions such as ADD or ADHD, asthma, diabetes, epilepsy, a heart condition, or Tourette's Disorder.


###
**Specific learning disability**

  * **General Definition** : A disorder involving psychological processes for understanding or using language, which can lead to difficulties in listening, thinking, speaking, reading, writing, spelling, or math, and adversely affects the child’s educational performance. Examples include dyslexia and dysgraphia.


###
**Speech or language impairment**

  * **General Definition** : A communication disorder, like stuttering, articulation issues, language impairment, or voice impairment, that adversely affects the child's educational performance. This may involve producing sounds, speech fluency, or understanding/using language.


###
**Traumatic brain injury**

  * **General Definition** : An acquired brain injury from an external physical force causing functional or psychosocial impairment that adversely affects the child's educational performance. It excludes congenital, degenerative, or birth-trauma-related brain injuries.


###
**Visual impairment**

  * **General Definition** : A vision impairment that, even with correction, adversely affects the child's educational performance. This includes both partial sight and blindness.

### **Can my student's Award Amount change?**

Yes. Based on program rules, your student's award amount may change before funding is issued depending on the educational setting you select.

For example:

  * Students attending a **private school** receive funding based on private school program guidelines
  * Students who are **homeschooled** receive funding based on homeschool program guidelines


Your student's final funding amount will be determined after you complete your educational setting selection and before funds are made available.

We recommend selecting your student's educational setting as soon as possible, as this directly impacts funding.

### **Why don't I see an Award Amount?**

You may not see an Award Amount if:

  * Your student has not yet been approved
  * Your application is still under review or in an appeals process
  * Your student is not eligible for funding


If your status changes to Approved, the Award Amount will appear automatically on your dashboard.

### **Does seeing an Award Amount mean I can access my wallet?**

No. Seeing an Award Amount does not mean funds are available yet.

Wallet access and spending will only be enabled after funding has been issued and when the program officially opens for use.

### **What should I do next?**

If you see an Award Amount:

  * Review your student's information
  * Complete any required steps (such as selecting a school or educational setting)
  * Watch for updates about when funding becomes available


If you have questions about your student's funding, please contact Odyssey Support.

### How Can Schools Become Eligible for the Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA) Program?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44036071350683-How-Can-Schools-Become-Eligible-for-the-Texas-Education-Freedom-Accounts-TEFA-Program*

_This article is intended to help Texas schools understand the eligibility requirements to participate in the TEFA Program._

To be eligible, a school must:

  * Be located in Texas. A virtual campus must have an administrative office located in Texas if they do not have a physical campus where instruction is provided.
  * Be accredited by an organization recognized by the Texas Private School Accreditation Commission or another accreditor approved by the Texas Education Agency.
  * Have operated a physical or virtual campus for at least two consecutive school years.
  * Agree to follow program requirements established by the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts.


Schools must also annually administer and provide the results of an approved nationally norm-referenced or state-approved assessment to participating TEFA students in grades 3-12.

Please note that if your school is **only** offering Pre-K classes _**for the purposes of TEFA,**_ only the following set of eligibility criteria applies:

To be eligible as a Pre-K Provider, you **must:**

  * Be eligible to contract with a school district to offer Pre-K
  * Be in good standing with the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services
  * Have not had action taken against your provider's license during the previous 24 months


Additionally, you **must meet one of the following criteria** :

  * Your school is a Texas Rising Star Program with a three- or four-star rating
  * Your school is a Texas School Ready participant
  * Your school possesses an existing partnership with a school district to _only_ offer Pre-K classes
  * Your school has a valid accreditation from an agency approved by the Commissioner of Education
  * Your school is accredited by an organization that is recognized by the Texas Private School Accreditation Commission

  3. **Compliance:** All vendor requirements still apply, including marketplace category approval, fingerprinting if applicable, and proper licensing for specialized services.


**Example:** A school providing traditional K-12 education can also offer test prep tutoring or online courses as a separate vendor offering, as long as these services are registered separately in the marketplace.

### Fingerprinting Requirements for Texas Vendors
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/46901528415387-Fingerprinting-Requirements-for-Texas-Vendors*

Texas requires fingerprinting for certain vendors and service providers who will have direct contact with students using TEFA funds.

**Who Needs Fingerprinting:**

  * Individual tutors and instructors who will teach students directly
  * Service providers offering in-person therapy or counseling
  * Staff members of larger vendors who interact directly with students
  * Any vendor or employee with unsupervised access to students


**Who Does NOT Need Fingerprinting:**

  * Vendors selling products only (books, supplies, software, etc.) with no direct student contact
  * Online instructors with no in-person interaction
  * Administrative or support personnel with no student contact


**Fingerprinting Process:**

  1. **Step 1:** Complete the vendor application in your Odyssey portal.
  2. **Step 2:** If required, you will be notified that fingerprinting is needed.
  3. **Step 3:** Schedule an appointment at a Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) office or approved fingerprinting location.
  4. **Step 4:** Provide fingerprints and submit results to the Odyssey platform.
  5. **Step 5:** Undergo a background check screening.
  6. **Step 6:** Once approved, your vendor status is activated.


**Cost:** Fingerprinting costs vary by location but typically range from $10-$25. Check your local DPS office for specific pricing.

**Timeline:** Background check results typically take 2-4 weeks after fingerprints are submitted.

**Important Note:** Any criminal convictions or disqualifying background information may result in vendor application denial. Consult TEFA program guidelines for specific disqualifying factors.

### When Are Professional Licenses Required in the TEFA Vendor Application?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/46602271890843-When-Are-Professional-Licenses-Required-in-the-TEFA-Vendor-Application*

  * **Medical Services:** Any medical assessment or intervention requires appropriate medical licensure (MD, DO, NP, PA, etc.).
  * **Instruction in Specialized Fields:** Some specialized instructional areas may require specific certifications or licenses depending on state requirements.


**Services NOT Requiring Licenses:**

  * General tutoring in academic subjects (without claiming teacher certification)
  * Music lessons and instruction
  * Art instruction
  * Sports and fitness coaching
  * Educational consulting and curriculum planning
  * Test preparation


**How to Include License Information:**

  1. When creating your vendor profile, you will have the option to list professional licenses and certifications.
  2. Provide your license number and issuing authority.
  3. The Odyssey team may verify licenses with the appropriate state board or credentialing body.
  4. License information will be visible to parents in your vendor profile, building trust and credibility.


**Important Reminders:**

  * Never claim certifications or licenses you do not hold.
  * Ensure all licenses are current and in good standing.
  * Renew licenses before expiration to maintain vendor status.
  * Report any license changes or suspensions to Odyssey immediately.


For questions about specific licensing requirements for your service type, contact TEFA support or the relevant Texas licensing board.

### How Can Out-of-State Vendors or Service Providers Qualify for Texas Education Freedom Accounts (TEFA)?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/43754731213595-How-Can-Out-of-State-Vendors-or-Service-Providers-Qualify-for-Texas-Education-Freedom-Accounts-TEFA*

Out-of-state vendors and service providers can participate in the TEFA Marketplace by meeting specific registration and qualification requirements set by the state of Texas.

**Eligibility for Out-of-State Vendors:**

  1. **Valid Business Entity:** Must have a legally recognized business entity in their home state.
  2. **Tax Compliance:** Must have a valid Federal Tax ID (EIN) and be in good standing with the IRS.
  3. **Service Type Match:** Services or products must fall within approved TEFA marketplace categories.
  4. **Background Check:** Subject to Texas background check requirements (fingerprinting if direct student contact).


**Registration Requirements for Out-of-State Entities:**

  1. **Foreign Entity Registration:** Must register as a foreign entity (out-of-state business) with the Texas Secretary of State if operating in Texas or serving Texas residents.
  2. **Registered Agent:** If required by Texas law, designate a registered agent with a physical address in Texas for legal service of process.
  3. **Texas Tax ID:** May need to obtain a Texas EIN or tax registration depending on the nature of services provided.
  4. **Business Address:** Provide a business address (may be virtual office or registered agent address) in Texas.


**Additional Documentation for Out-of-State Vendors:**

  * Certificate of Good Standing from home state
  * Business license or proof of business registration from home state
  * Articles of incorporation or organization (if applicable)
  * Authorization to conduct business in Texas (if required by state)
  * Proof of professional licenses (if applicable in service area)


**Verification Process:**

  1. Submit vendor application through Odyssey Marketplace
  2. Provide proof of out-of-state business registration
  3. Upload Texas foreign entity registration or documents showing compliance with Texas requirements
  4. Submit to background check and verification process
  5. Odyssey team reviews all documentation
  6. Upon approval, vendor is activated in marketplace


**Services Available to Out-of-State Vendors:**

  * **Distance Learning:** Online courses, tutoring, and instruction
  * **Virtual Services:** Counseling, therapy, coaching (via telehealth)
  * **Product Sales:** Books, curriculum, educational materials (shipped to Texas)
  * **Consulting Services:** Curriculum planning, assessment, instructional design


**In-Person Services:** Out-of-state providers offering in-person services in Texas must comply with all local, state, and federal requirements, including professional licensing and background checks specific to their service type.

**Important Considerations:**

  * Out-of-state professional licenses (tutors, therapists, etc.) may not be automatically recognized in Texas. Verify licensing requirements with the appropriate Texas licensing board.
  * Telehealth services are subject to state-specific regulations and may require Texas licensure.
  * Vendors must comply with all Texas tax requirements even if foreign entity registered.
  * Legal service of process requirements must be met if registered as a foreign entity.


For specific questions about out-of-state vendor registration and requirements, contact TEFA support or consult with a legal professional familiar with Texas business requirements.

### Can Public Schools be Vendors?
*Source: https://support.withodyssey.com/hc/en-us/articles/44030231575195-Can-Public-Schools-be-Vendors*

Yes, public schools can become vendors and offer services and programs through the TEFA Marketplace, with important restrictions and considerations.

**Key Requirement:** Services offered by public schools must not count toward average daily attendance (ADA) for funding purposes. This means public schools can offer supplemental services but cannot count TEFA-funded programs as part of their primary educational offering or ADA calculations.

**Eligible Services for Public School Vendors:**

  * Tutoring and supplemental academic instruction (offered outside regular school day)
  * Summer school programs (for TEFA funds)
  * Specialized services (counseling, speech therapy, occupational therapy)
  * Enrichment and extracurricular programs
  * Technology services or equipment
  * Professional development for educators or parents
  * Facility rentals or space sharing


**Services NOT Eligible:**

  * Full-time K-12 curriculum delivery for ADA funding
  * Programs that would count toward student attendance requirements
  * Services funded by regular public education appropriations
  * Programs that replace or substitute for public school services


**Compliance Requirements:**

  1. Clearly document that services do not count toward ADA.
  2. Maintain separate accounting and billing for TEFA services.
  3. Ensure services do not create conflicts with public school mission or funding.
  4. Comply with all state regulations regarding public school fund usage.
  5. Obtain necessary approvals from district administration before listing offerings.


**Why This Restriction Exists:** The restriction ensures that TEFA funds are used to supplement education rather than shift funding from public to public schools. It protects the public education system while allowing schools to offer additional valuable services to families using TEFA accounts.

**Application Process for Public Schools:**

  1. Contact your school district administration about becoming a TEFA vendor.
  2. Ensure proposed services comply with ADA and public fund restrictions.
  3. Submit vendor application through Odyssey Marketplace.
  4. Provide documentation of district approval.
  5. Detail services to be offered and how they will not affect ADA.
  6. Complete background check and approval process.


**Important Note:** Public schools should consult with their district legal counsel and state education agency to ensure full compliance with all regulations before offering services through TEFA.
